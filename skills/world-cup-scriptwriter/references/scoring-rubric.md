# Scoring Rubric

Score before writing. Reject weak ideas early.

## Topic Preflight

Run this before a topic card appears in the UI.

| Dimension | Max | High Score Means |
|---|---:|---|
| 钩子张力 | 25 | There is a visible incident, replay reveal, pressure point, conflict, or comment-side debate |
| 口语感 | 15 | The title and hook can be read aloud without sounding like a report |
| 事实锚点 | 20 | It includes a scoreline, player, visible action, replay clue, crowd reaction, rule condition, or old fact |
| 互动性 | 15 | The viewer knows what to watch, predict, or argue about |
| 中文本地化 | 10 | Chinese output uses Chinese team and player names |
| 素材支撑 | 15 | There are official/highlight/full-match/press assets to support it |

Topic verdict:

- `pass`: 82+
- `repair`: 65-81, repair once and rescore
- `hide`: below 65, do not show in UI

Repair rules:

- Material weak -> supplement official/highlight/full-match/press searches before rewriting.
- Hook weak -> attach the topic to a visible incident, replay reveal, human relationship, or comment-side question.
- Too objective -> add a viewer action: watch the first shot, first counterattack, face, crowd, replay clue, or comment side.
- English team/player names in Chinese output -> localize names before scoring again.
- If the repaired title duplicates a stronger title, skip it instead of showing duplicates.

## Content Pack Dimensions

Use these dimensions after a topic has passed preflight and before generating/editing a final video.

| Dimension | Max | High Score Means |
|---|---:|---|
| Audience clarity | 15 | The viewer is obvious: beginner, debate seeker, emotion fan, star fan, lifestyle user |
| Three-second hook | 15 | The first line has a visible incident, record, conflict, pressure, replay clue, or contrast |
| Protagonist/story | 15 | There is a clear player, keeper, referee, team, or viewer identity |
| Fact anchor | 15 | There is a scoreline, age, record, minute, single action, or rule change |
| Visual proof | 12 | There is a highlight, official clip, press video, face, crowd, or diagram |
| Human voiceover | 12 | The line sounds spoken, not like a research memo |
| Editing rhythm | 8 | 0-3s conclusion, evidence within 10s, no slow setup |
| Interaction hook | 8 | The ending asks viewers to choose, predict, argue, or add examples |
| Remixability | 7 | A line, face, nickname, meme, or template can be reused |

## Verdict

- `go`: 85+
- `revise`: 70-84
- `skip`: below 70

## Validation Questions

- Can a non-fan explain the point after watching?
- Does the first sentence create tension?
- Is there a concrete shot for every voiceover beat?
- Does the ending create a comment?
- Can the video be made without unauthorized match footage if needed?
- Is there one protagonist and one concrete fact?
- Would the comment section have a reusable phrase or side to take?
